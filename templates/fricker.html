Module 05 — Social Epistemology

Epistemic Injustice

Miranda Fricker (2007) argues that people can be wronged specifically as knowers — not just as people. Explore testimonial and hermeneutical injustice through real-world scenarios.

Select a scenario. Identify the type of epistemic injustice, the credibility deficit or hermeneutical gap, and Fricker's analysis of the harm.

🗣
Testimonial Injustice
A speaker receives a credibility deficit due to identity prejudice in the hearer. Wronged in the exchange of knowledge.
🔇
Hermeneutical Injustice
A gap in collective interpretive resources prevents understanding one's own experience. Structural, non-agential wrong.
